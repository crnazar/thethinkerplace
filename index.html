About

Profile Photo

Christina Restrepo Nazar, Ph.D.

Doctoral Student | Texts & Technology | University of Central Florida

My journey in education began as a K-12 science teacher, where I witnessed firsthand how students' cultural backgrounds and lived experiences shape their learning. This sparked my passion for equity-oriented, youth-centered approaches to education.

I earned my Ph.D. from Michigan State University, where I developed the "Youth as Teacher Educators" framework. I then served as Assistant Professor of Science Education at California State University, Los Angeles, leading The T(h)inker Place research lab.

Now at UCF, I'm exploring new frontiers: AI as a cultural borderland, drawing on Gloria Anzald√∫a's transformative theories to understand how we navigate digital spaces and create meaning at the intersections of technology and culture.
