About

Profile Photo

Christina Restrepo Nazar, Ph.D.

Ph.D. Student, Texts & Technology | Graduate Research Associate, CHDR | University of Central Florida

My research spans teacher education, STEM education, digital learning, and artificial intelligence, grounded in a commitment to understanding how devoted engagement with learning transforms both educators and students. I focus on enhancing the preparation of future educators to teach science and engineering through approaches that honor the complexity of human understanding and growth.

I earned my first Ph.D. from Michigan State University (2018), where I developed the nationally recognized "Youth as Teacher Educators" framework, winning the AACTE Outstanding Dissertation Award. I then served as Assistant Professor of Science Education at California State University, Los Angeles (2018-2023).

Now pursuing a second doctorate at UCF in Texts & Technology with a specialization in Digital Humanities and AI, I examine how digital systems shape scientific and technical communication across learning environments‚Äîasking how we might design technologies that preserve and amplify the human dimensions of growth and understanding.
